How the RSA Algorithm Works

For RSA Algorithm, there needs to be 3 numbers created: n, e, and d. From these numbers, the Public key is (e, n) and Private key is (d, n). While n is the same for the Public and Private keys, d is practically impossible to derive from e, making the private key secure. Idealy for realistically secure keys, the prime numbers used would be very large, so that the n value is almost impossible to factor to find the p and q values used to get to the other values (e and d) used in the keys.

Below, there are steps that walk through creating both the public and private keys, with a simple example that shows the process. It also includes how to then use the keys to encrypt and decrypt a message.

  • Step 1: Choose p and q (both as prime numbers):

    \(p=5, q=11\)

  • Step 2: Compute n, where \(n=p \cdot q\):

    \(n=5\cdot 11 = 55\)

  • Step 3: Compute \(r= (p-1)*(q-1)\):

    \(r = (5-1)\cdot (11-1) = 4\cdot 10=40\)

  • Step 4: Choose e where \( 1 < e < r \), with \(e\) and \(r\) are coprime (meaning \(gcd(e, r)=1\)):

    \(1 < e < 40\), if \( e = 7 \), \( gcd(7,40) = 1 \)

  • Step 5: Compute d where \( (d \cdot e) \pmod r = 1 \):

    \((d\cdot 7)\equiv 1 \pmod{40}\), one solution is \(d=23\), \(23\cdot 7 \pmod{40} = 1\)

Now: Values of e, d, and n have been determined, so Public key = (7, 55) and Private key = (23, 55).

Using these keys, encrypt and decrypt message "HI". Convert the letters first to numbers (using their number in the alphabet) H = 8, I = 9. So message being encrypted will be represented as 89. Since 89 is larger than n (55), we have to split it into smaller pieces, so encrypt 8 and 9 separately.

Formula for Encryption: \(m^e \pmod{n}\), where m is the message.
Formula for Decryption: \(c^d \pmod{n}\), where c is the encrypted message.

Encryption: \(m_1 = 8\): \(8^7 \pmod{55}= 2\), \(m_2 = 9\): \(9^7 \pmod{55}= 4\)
Decryption: \(c_1 = 2\): \(2^{23} \pmod{55}=8\), \(c_2 = 4\): \(4^{23} \pmod{55}=9\)
